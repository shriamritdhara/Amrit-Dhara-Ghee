How It's Made

The Ancient Bilona Process

Four steps. Centuries of tradition. The only right way to make ghee.

01
🐄

Ethical Milking

Milk is collected from happy, freely-grazed A2 desi cows only after their calves are fed — respecting the natural bond.

02
🥛

Curd Culturing

Fresh A2 milk is set into curd overnight using traditional starter cultures — slow, natural, and full of probiotics.

03
🪵

Bilona Churning

The curd is slow-churned with a wooden bilona in both directions to separate pure golden butter — a labour of love, not machinery.

04
🔥

Slow Simmering

Butter is simmered on a low flame in clay pots until pure liquid gold remains — clarified to aromatic perfection.

Why A2

What Makes A2 Ghee
Different

A2 ghee comes from cows that produce only the A2 beta-casein protein in their milk — unlike commercial breeds that produce A1 protein, which many people find hard to digest.

Indigenous Indian breeds like Gir, Sahiwal, and Tharparkar are naturally A2 cows. Their milk — and the ghee made from it — is closer to what the Ayurvedic tradition has celebrated for millennia.

Combined with the bilona method, our ghee retains butyric acid, fat-soluble vitamins, and conjugated linoleic acid (CLA) that industrial processing destroys.
